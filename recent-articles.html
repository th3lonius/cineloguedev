Bronson

by Mark Mesaros on 27 August 2013

Bronson’s obvious machismo combined with the gunplay implies a latent sexuality, but Refn spends the remainder of the film sculpting a character who couldn’t be more asexual, at times resembling an impotent steroid abuser or a listless and impotent heroin fiend. It’s simply the first of many red herrings in a film that many will observe resembles Kubrick’s A Clockwork Orange a little more than superficially.
